PageRank Algorithm

The PageRank algorithm is probably my favority algorithm of them all. This is the algorithm that is used by Google searches to rank websites in their search engine results. When you enter in a search word or phrase in Google and click "search", you will most likely find the desired web link within the first few links that are returned (and most definitely on the first page of the results). This is all made possible by the PageRank algorithm. Before Google, it was common for somone to click on page after page of results from a search engine to find a desired web link. Think about it...when was the last time that you actually clicked on "page 2" of the results from a Google search?

In sort, PageRank is a way of measuring the importance of website pages. PageRank was named after Larry Page - one of the founders of Google. "PageRank works by counting the number and quality of links to a page to determine a rough estimate of how important the website is. The underlying assumption is that more important websites are likely to receive more links from other websites." (source) PageRank is not the only algorithm used by Google currently uses to order search results, but it is however the very first algorithm used by Google and it is also the best-known.
